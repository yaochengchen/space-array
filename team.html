Collaboration

An international community for space interferometry.

Space Array brings together scientists, engineers, institutions, agencies, industry partners, students, and early-career researchers working toward a future distributed astronomical observatory.

Consortium structure

A collaboration spanning science, engineering, and mission architecture.

The initiative can be organized around complementary roles: science definition, mission architecture, enabling technology, data systems, institutional coordination, and community development.

Science

Science working groups

Define science drivers, target classes, observing requirements, simulations, and science traceability for a future space array.

Architecture

Mission concept team

Explore candidate orbits, spacecraft configurations, baseline evolution, observing modes, operations concepts, and pathfinder missions.

Technology

Enabling technology leads

Develop and assess timing, metrology, receivers, optical links, onboard storage, correlation, calibration, and imaging technologies.

Data systems

Correlation and imaging contributors

Build simulation tools, synthetic observation pipelines, calibration strategies, sparse-array imaging methods, and science-ready data products.

Institutions

Partner institutes and agencies

Support studies, workshops, proposal development, technical reviews, infrastructure planning, and international coordination.

Community

Early-career researchers and students

Contribute to simulations, analysis, instrument studies, outreach, workshops, and the long-term development of the Space Array community.

Ways to contribute

Multiple paths into the Space Array community.

Space Array is inherently interdisciplinary. Scientists, engineers, software developers, students, agencies, and industry partners can contribute at different stages of the initiative.

Science

Define science cases

Help refine target classes, observing requirements, simulation studies, and science-to-system traceability.

Engineering

Develop enabling technologies

Contribute expertise in formation flying, timing, metrology, receivers, optical links, spacecraft systems, and mission design.

Data systems

Build analysis pipelines

Support simulation, correlation, calibration, sparse-array imaging, dynamic reconstruction, and science-ready data products.

Community

Organize workshops and studies

Help coordinate meetings, white papers, proposal activities, student projects, and international collaboration.
