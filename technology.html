Technology roadmap

From enabling technologies to an operational array.

The Space Array initiative connects science requirements to the technologies needed for formation-flying interferometry in space.

System architecture

An observatory built from synchronized spacecraft, links, and computation.

Space Array depends on more than individual spacecraft. It requires a coordinated architecture in which formation flying, timing, receivers, optical links, correlation, and imaging operate as a single interferometric instrument.

The technology roadmap therefore connects physical spacecraft capabilities to the information flow needed for science: collecting signals, preserving phase, transporting data, reconstructing baselines, and producing images.

Architecture flow

From astronomical signal to science-ready image.

A space interferometer must preserve information across many steps: collecting radiation, maintaining phase coherence, transporting data, reconstructing baselines, correlating signals, and producing interpretable science products.

01

Sky signal

Compact astronomical targets emit millimetre or sub-millimetre radiation.

02

Spacecraft receivers

Distributed spacecraft collect the signal across long baselines.

03

Timing & baseline knowledge

Clock, orbit, and metrology systems preserve the information needed for phase calibration.

04

Data transport

High-rate links and downlink systems move data to processing infrastructure.

05

Correlation

Signals from separated spacecraft are combined into interferometric measurements.

06

Image reconstruction

Calibration and imaging pipelines turn sparse measurements into science products.
