Candidate pathfinder mission

SHARP: a pathfinder for space-based black-hole imaging.

SHARP is a proposed ESA M-class mission concept for a three-satellite sub-millimetre space-VLBI interferometer, focused on demonstrating the core technologies needed for future space-based black-hole imaging.

Focused pathfinder

A concrete step toward space-based very-long-baseline interferometry.

SHARP can be framed as a focused pathfinder for sub-millimetre space VLBI: compact enough to define measurable requirements, but ambitious enough to test the observing chain needed for a future Space Array observatory.

Its role is to connect black-hole imaging goals to evolving space baselines, high-frequency receivers, GNSS and laser-based relative navigation, optical inter-satellite links, onboard real-time correlation, and ground-based calibration and imaging.

Mission concept parameters

Connecting black-hole science goals to system-level requirements.

SHARP can be described through a small set of mission parameters that link scientific ambition to architecture, operations, and technology demonstrations.

Science target

Black-hole imaging

Focused high-resolution observations of event-horizon-scale structure, accretion flows, photon-ring signatures, and jet-launching regions.

Reference architecture

Three-satellite sub-mm interferometer

A compact space-VLBI configuration using three spacecraft to form multiple evolving baselines for high-frequency interferometric observations.

Orbit concept

Evolving MEO baselines

Slightly different orbits provide changing baseline geometry and uv coverage, reducing reliance on fuel-intensive precision formation flying.

Relative navigation

GNSS and laser ranging

Relative spacecraft positions are reconstructed through orbit determination, GNSS-based navigation, and laser ranging to support interferometric calibration.

Data system

Optical links and onboard correlation

High-rate raw data are exchanged through optical inter-satellite links, correlated onboard in real time, and downlinked as compact correlated data products.

Path to Space Array

Architecture validation

SHARP would reduce risk for a larger Space Array by validating receivers, baseline reconstruction, links, correlation, calibration, and imaging before scaling up.
